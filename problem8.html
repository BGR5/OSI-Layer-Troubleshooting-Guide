Problem #8: Destination Host Unreachable

Performing a CMD ping test can measure your latency, check the network connectivity of your computer, and show the quality of your connection. However, many users encounter the "Destination Host Unreachable" error during these tests. This typically indicates there is no route to the destination.

If the error simply says “Destination host unreachable”, it means your PC cannot find a route to the destination. If it says "Reply from [IP]: Destination host unreachable", then there's likely a routing issue on the remote side.

How to Fix:

  1. Check Your Internet Connection

    Open your browser and visit a known website like google.com. If it loads successfully, your internet is fine, and the issue lies within your local network.

  2. Test Your IPv6 Connection

    If IPv6 has issues, it can also trigger this error.
    Step 1: Press Win + R, type cmd, and hit Enter.
    Step 2: In the CMD window, run: ping -6 192.168.1.1
    Step 3: If the error persists, use: tracert [IP address] to trace the route.
    Step 4: If it still fails, check the default gateway settings by typing: netsh → interface ipv6 → show config

  3. Add the Right Gateway Address Manually

    If the default gateway is incorrect or missing, add it manually:
    Step 1: Open Settings → Network & Internet
    Step 2: Choose Ethernet from the sidebar
    Step 3: Click Change adapter options
    Step 4: Right-click your network > Properties
    Step 5: Select Internet Protocol Version 6 (TCP/IPv6) > click Properties
    Step 6: Enter the correct Default Gateway and save. Restart your PC and test again.
